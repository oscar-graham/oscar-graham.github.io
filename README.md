# Squirrel Competition Simulator
This project was made as part of my Communicating Biomedical Science and Engineering module at Imperial College

The simulation aims to teach concepts of Competition and Adaptation using the example of red and grey squirrels. The squirrels compete for food and shelter while trying to avoid being eaten by fox and sparrowhawk predators. The grey squirrels have a higher reproduction rate and are less likely to be spotted by predators. The grey squirrels also carry the squirrelpox virus which can harm the red squirrels.

The simulation contains three different environments: Ancient Woodland, Fragmented Parkland and Urban Fringe.
- Ancient Woodland: Both food and shelter are plentiful, so both red and grey squirrels can maintain their population as there are enough resources to go around.
- Fragmented Parkland: Food and shelter are reduced, red and grey squirrels must compete more strongly for the limited resources. The adaptations of grey squirrels mean that they tend to survive for longer than the red squirrels.
- Urban Fringe: Food and shelter are further reduced. There is no longer enough shelter for either red or grey squirrels to hide from predators so they are quickly eaten up by the predators.

The initial number of squirrels and predators can be set at the start of the simulation, and the speed slider can be used to speed up or slow down the simulation. The population overtime graphic provides a live graph of the relative populations of the squirrels and predators, allowing population trends to be interpreted more easily.
